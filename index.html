WAM-Nav: Asymmetric Latent World-Action Modeling for Unified Visual Navigation

Anonymous
Anonymous Institution
Video

WAM-Nav enables proactive, foresight-driven visual navigation across image-goal, point-goal, and no-goal tasks with zero-shot sim-to-real transfer.

Abstract

Visual navigation requires generating smooth and collision-free trajectories under complex geometric and physical constraints. Existing reactive policies that directly map observations to actions lack anticipatory reasoning, limiting their ability to proactively avoid obstacles. While visual imagination offers predictive foresight, conventional modular approaches separate scene prediction from policy learning, often leading to error accumulation and inefficient inference.

To address these limitations, we propose WAM-Nav, a Latent World-Action Model for embodied visual navigation that jointly learns action generation and latent future visual prediction, enabling more robust and foresighted navigation decisions without compromising inference efficiency. Specifically, WAM-Nav utilizes a shared Diffusion Transformer for asymmetric joint diffusion to concurrently generate long-horizon actions and short-horizon visual foresight, reducing the inference latency and visual error accumulation inherent in multi-step autoregressive rollouts.

To further encourage smooth and consistent trajectory generation, we introduce a dual-stream contextual conditioning mechanism that integrates episode-level ego-motion history with sequential visual observations. Combined with a unified goal alignment module that preserves balanced representations across goal types, WAM-Nav naturally supports image-goal, point-goal, and no-goal navigation within a single policy. Extensive experiments on the challenging ClutterScenes and InternScenes benchmarks demonstrate strong generalization of WAM-Nav, particularly on image-goal and point-goal navigation, where it improves success rates by 15.7% and 3.3%, respectively. Real-world deployment further validates effective zero-shot sim-to-real transfer, achieving an average 85% task success rate across diverse indoor and outdoor environments.

Approach

WAM-Nav consists of three key components that jointly enable unified, foresight-driven visual navigation:

(1) Unified Goal Alignment. Heterogeneous navigation goals are projected into complementary visual-semantic and geometric queries, preserving modality-specific information while enabling balanced performance across image-goal, point-goal, and no-goal exploration within a single policy.

(2) Dual-Stream Contextual Conditioning (DSCC). A goal-modulated visual memory stream captures local spatial geometry from historical RGB observations, while a trajectory-aware motion history stream encodes kinematic momentum from episode-level ego-motion. The fused context condition C constrains subsequent generation to be both geometrically safe and kinematically smooth.

(3) Asymmetric Action-Foresight Generation. Conditioned on C, a shared Diffusion Transformer performs flow-matching-based asymmetric joint diffusion: long-horizon action trajectories are generated together with short-horizon latent visual foresight in a compact representation space. This design couples perception and control while avoiding the latency and error accumulation of multi-step autoregressive visual rollouts.

During deployment, WAM-Nav operates in a receding-horizon control loop: candidate trajectories are sampled via an Euler ODE solver, and the initial action segment is executed at each step, balancing closed-loop reactivity with long-horizon spatiotemporal foresight.

Training & Benchmarks

WAM-Nav is trained on the large-scale visual navigation dataset VLN-N1, built upon six categories of 3D scene assets (Replica, Matterport3D, Gibson, 3D-FRONT, HSSD, and HM3D) with over 400 hours and 200K+ collision-free trajectories. We evaluate zero-shot generalization on IsaacSim using ClearPath Dingo across ClutterScenes (easy/hard) and InternScenes (home/commercial), covering image-goal, point-goal, and no-goal navigation with 6,000 episodes in total.

Evaluation in Simulation

Compared with strong baselines (GNM, ViNT, NoMaD, NWM, NavDP, and others), WAM-Nav achieves the best average performance across all three tasks: 50.2% SR / 48.2% SPL on image-goal, 80.4% SR / 78.0% SPL on point-goal, and 168.9 m² explored area in no-goal exploration. Qualitative results show that WAM-Nav proactively avoids obstacles and produces smoother, more consistent trajectories than reactive policies, with decoded latent foresight closely aligned to ground-truth observations.

Zero-Shot Real-World Deployment

We deploy WAM-Nav zero-shot on a Unitree G1 humanoid robot with an Intel RealSense D455 camera across four representative environments: meeting room, warehouse, lobby, and parking lot. WAM-Nav consistently predicts feasible trajectories under diverse layouts and lighting conditions, achieving higher success rates and stronger robustness than NavDP in real-world trials.
